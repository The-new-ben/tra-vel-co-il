# Affiliate Travel Guidelines

Updated: 2026-05-27

## Disclosure Standard

Every page with an affiliate, sponsored, or supplier-referral link must show clear Hebrew disclosure before or near the offer.

Approved disclosure:

> גילוי מסחרי: אם תזמינו דרך קישור או ספק שמופיע בעמוד ייתכן שנקבל עמלה. המחיר, הזמינות והתנאים נקבעים על ידי הספק ויש לבדוק אותם לפני רכישה.

## Deal Page Requirements

- Update date.
- Supplier/source name.
- What is included and not included.
- Baggage/cancellation/change terms.
- City tax/resort fee/extra local costs where relevant.
- Insurance and medical coverage warnings where relevant.
- Visa/entry rule verification note where relevant.
- Clear statement that price and availability can change.

## Evidence Levels

- `Supplier` - information from partner/supplier.
- `Observed` - price or availability observed on a date.
- `Editorial` - destination or itinerary recommendation.
- `User request` - lead-specific planning, not public offer.

## Do Not Publish

- Fake "deal" pages without current offer data.
- Guarantees of price, availability, visa entry, refund, or insurance payout.
- Thin affiliate pages with no itinerary, practical route detail, or decision help.
- Vacation rental schema without verified listing data and Google eligibility.

## First Content Templates

- City break page: when to go, flight airports, best areas to sleep, 3-day itinerary, family/couple notes, package CTA, insurance CTA, eSIM CTA.
- Insurance page: who needs it, what to check, common exclusions, pre-existing conditions note, cancellation/shortening trip note.
- Cheap flights page: flexible dates, baggage traps, airport transfer costs, night flights, cancellation terms.
